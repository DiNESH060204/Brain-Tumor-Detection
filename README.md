# Brain Tumor Detection using Comparative Edge Detection Operators

Abstract - Brain tumors, whether malignant or benign,
pose significant challenge in the medical field due to
their often-elusive nature and the severe implications
they carry. Diagnosing these tumors accurately
requires the expertise of doctor’s specialists, who rely
on a combination of advanced imaging techniques and
clinical evaluations. Machine learning has emerged as
a powerful ally in this battle, particularly in the realm
of edge detection and image analysis. Various edge
detection operators, such as the Sobel, Canny, and
Kirsch operators, play a crucial role in enhancing the
clarity of MRI scans, making it easier to delineate the
tumor boundaries. This paper conducts a comparative
analysis of various operators to determine their
efficacy in detecting brain tumors, thereby
highlighting the potential of machine learning to
significantly improve diagnostic accuracy and
treatment outcomes.
Keywords—Brain Tumor, Diagnosis, Machine
learning, MRI scans, edge detection operators, Accuracy,
Outcomes


Introduction:

Brain tumors are complex and often fatal, requiring preci
se diagnostics. Machine learning, especially edge detection
operators like Sobel, Canny, and Kirsch, enhances MRI anal
ysis by better outlining tumors. Pradeep Kumar Mallick, et
al. [1], comparison between various operators were done for
edge detection of a tumor/cyst in brain, following which the
research terminates with distance transform calculation, and
claims that Canny operators performs best than all others.
SangeethaG, et al. [2], provides insights to edge detection
techniques to extract regions of the brain from MRI scans.
Sobel and Prewitt operators are used to detect edges. Further
morphological operations are used to make up for the erosion
and dilation of unwanted parts.D. Haritha, compares between
various brain tumor detection techniques alternately
combining morphological operations, K-means algorithm,
Local Binary Pattern, edge detection and more,
simultaneously. The paper also gives the time measure taken
by each of these techniques.[3] Warqaa Shaher AlAzawee, et
al., taken Lenna’s image and a noisy fingerprint image, they
are seen to apply the concept of erosion, wherein, results are 
compared across operators like, Sobel, Prewit, Canny and
Cellular Automata. It’s seen that disk-shaped morphological
operators are used to identify fine edges in binary images
produced through Otsu’s thresholding method. Remya Ajai
A S, et al, [2022], taken Kaggle’s Brain Tumor Dataset, its
proven that 8-Sobel detection was the most suitable for tumor
detection in brain, then the original Sobel, Prewitt, Roberts,
Canny and Laplacian of Gaussian (LoG) operators.
K.Srisabarimani, et al., [8], discusses about edge detection,
noise reduction and feature extraction and other soft
computing techniques used in traditional edge detection
mechanisms.
